## 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 4.1 Standard CDD Requirements

For ALL customers, we verify:

**Individual Directors/Shareholders:**
- Full legal name (as per passport/ID)
- Date of birth
- Nationality and country of residence
- Residential address (proof required)
- Government-issued ID (passport, driving license, national ID)

**Proof of Address (issued within 3 months):**
- Bank statement
- Utility bill
- Council tax bill
- Official government letter

**Proof of Identity:**
- Passport (biometric page)
- National ID card (both sides)
- UK driving license (photocard)

### 4.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Required when customer is:

**Politically Exposed Person (PEP):**
- Current or former senior government official
- Senior political party member
- High-ranking military officer
- Board member of state-owned enterprise
- Immediate family members of PEPs

**EDD Measures for PEPs:**
- Senior management approval
- Source of wealth verification
- Source of funds verification
- Ongoing monitoring (quarterly reviews)
- Adverse media screening

**High-Risk Jurisdictions:**
- FATF blacklisted countries
- EU high-risk third countries
- Sanctions list countries (OFAC, UN, EU)

**Complex Corporate Structures:**
- Multiple layers of ownership
- Offshore companies involved
- Trusts or nominee arrangements
- Unusually complex shareholding

**EDD Documentation:**
- Certified financial statements
- Bank reference letters
- Proof of business activity
- Explanation of fund sources

### 4.3 Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO)

We identify individuals who:
- Own >25% shares or voting rights
- Exercise significant control
- Have right to appoint/remove directors

**UBO Verification:**
- Full CDD on all UBOs
- Ownership structure chart
- Declaration of beneficial ownership

## 5. Ongoing Monitoring

**Continuous Activities:**

**Annual Reviews:**
- Update customer information
- Re-verify identity documents
- Review transaction patterns
- Check sanctions lists

**Transaction Monitoring:**
- Unusual payment patterns
- Large cash transactions

- Transactions inconsistent with business

**Red Flags:**
- Reluctance to provide information
- Frequent address changes
- Use of multiple bank accounts

Check ALL customers against:

**Automated Screening:**

**Positive Matches:**
- Transaction blocked immediately

- Senior management review
- Potential SAR filing

## 8. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

### 8.1 When to File a SAR

Report if you know, suspect, or have reasonable grounds to suspect:

**Money Laundering:**
- Funds derived from criminal activity
- Concealing criminal property
- Transferring criminal proceeds
- Acquiring, using, or possessing criminal funds

**Terrorist Financing:**
- Funding terrorism or terrorist organizations
- Facilitating terrorist acts

### 8.2 SAR Filing Process

**Internal Process:**
1. Employee identifies suspicious activity
2. Complete internal SAR form
3. Submit to MLRO within 24 hours
4. MLRO reviews within 48 hours
5. MLRO decides on NCA submission

**External Reporting (if MLRO approves):**
- Submit SAR to National Crime Agency (NCA)
- Use SAR Online system
- Within 7 days of knowledge/suspicion
- Request Defense Against Money Laundering (DAML) if needed

**Tipping Off:**
- **PROHIBITED:** Informing customer of SAR
- Criminal offense (up to 5 years imprisonment)
- Applies to all staff, not just MLRO

## 12. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

### 12.1 PEP Definition

**Domestic PEPs (UK):**
- Members of Parliament (MPs)
- Government ministers
- Senior civil servants (Grade 5+)
- Senior military officers
- High court judges
- Senior police officers
- Board members of state enterprises

**Foreign PEPs:**
- Heads of state/government
- Ministers and deputy ministers
- Members of parliament
- Senior judicial officials
- Senior military officials
- State-owned enterprise executives
- Political party officials

**International Organization PEPs:**
- UN senior officials
- EU commissioners
- World Bank/IMF executives

**Family Members:**
- Spouse or civil partner
- Children and their spouses
- Parents

**Close Associates:**
- Business partners
- Close social/professional relationships

### 12.2 PEP Procedures

**Identification:**
- Automated PEP screening (LexisNexis, World-Check)
- Self-declaration form
- Media and internet searches

**Approval:**
- Senior management approval REQUIRED
- Documented risk assessment
- Justification for business relationship

**Monitoring:**
- Quarterly transaction reviews
- Annual enhanced CDD refresh
- Ongoing media monitoring

**Exit:**
- PEP status remains for 12 months after leaving position
- Reduced monitoring after 12 months
- Full exit after 24 months (case-by-case)

## 13. Sanctions Compliance

**Prohibited Activities:**

**Asset Freeze:**
- Cannot form companies for sanctioned individuals/entities
- Cannot provide services to sanctioned parties
- Must freeze and report sanctioned assets

**Trade Restrictions:**
- No services for sanctioned sectors (e.g., Russian oil, North Korean weapons)
- Dual-use goods restrictions

**Financial Restrictions:**
- No payments to/from sanctioned jurisdictions
- No correspondent banking for sanctioned entities

**Licensing:**
- Apply to OFSI (Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation) if required
