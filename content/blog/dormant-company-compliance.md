# Dormant Company Maintenance: Staying Compliant for £0

## Introduction: The "Strategic Pause"
A **Dormant Company** is an entity that has had "no significant accounting transactions" during its financial year. Founders often keep a company dormant to protect a brand name, secure a future business idea, or pause operations during a strategic pivot. However, "Dormant" does not mean "Ignored."

## 1. The Legal DNA of Inactivity
Even if your company is not trading, it remains a legal entity on the Companies House register. You MUST maintain its "Good Standing" to avoid automatic strike-offs or personal fines.

### Mandatory Filings:
- **Dormant Accounts (AA02)**: A simplified balance sheet showing the company is inactive.
- **Confirmation Statement (CS01)**: An annual confirmation of directors, PSCs, and registered office details.

## 2. Avoiding the "Penalty Trap"
HMRC and Companies House treat compliance deadlines with absolute robotic precision.
- **Late Accounts**: Fines start at £150 and escalate to £1,500 for a private company.
- **Ignored CS01**: Can lead to the company being struck off the register, with all assets (including bank balances and intellectual property) becoming the property of the Crown (*Bona Vacantia*).
