# Registered Office vs. Service Address: Your Premium Privacy Shield

## The Privacy Imperative in UK Formation
For many directors, the most significant risk in incorporating a UK Limited company is the involuntary disclosure of their home address. Under UK law, every company must have a **Registered Office (RO)**, and every director must provide a **Service Address (SA)**. If managed incorrectly, these details are published on the global Companies House register, eternally searchable by the public.

## 1. The Registered Office (RO): The Corporate Anchor
The RO is the official legal address of the company. It is where government correspondence (HMRC, Companies House) is sent. 
- **Risk**: Using your home address as the RO means your residence is listed as the business headquarters.
- **Solution**: A prestigious, non-residential address (e.g., our London, SE1 location) projects professionalism and anchors your firm in a global financial hub.

## 2. The Service Address (SA): The Director's Shield
The SA is the official correspondence address for an individual director or PSC. 
- **The Gap**: Many founders realize too late that the SA and RO are two distinct requirements.
- **The Protection**: Using a professional Service Address allows you to keep your residential address on the 'protected' registry, visible only to law enforcement and credit reference agencies.
