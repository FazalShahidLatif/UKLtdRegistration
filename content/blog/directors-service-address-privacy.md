# Director's Service Address: Protecting Your Privacy in the UK

## The Director's Public Profile

When you become a director of a UK company, you effectively step onto a public stage. While transparency is a hallmark of the UK business environment, individual privacy is a fundamental right.

Navigating the balance between accountability and privacy involves understanding the **Director's Service Address**. This guide explains how you can protect your personal residence from the public record while remaining fully compliant with UK law.

---

## 1. Service Address vs. Residential Address

Every director is required to provide two addresses to Companies House:
1.  **Residential Address**: Where you actually live. This is kept on a private register accessible only to law enforcement and certain credit reference agencies.
2.  **Service Address**: The official address used for legal correspondence. **This is public.**

By default, many directors use their home address for both. This is a mistake.

### Absolute Privacy

### Global Flexibility

### Credibility

## 3. Legal Obligations and Compliance

A Director's Service Address is where official legal documents (like a "claim form" if the company is sued) are served. If you use a service address, the provider must be reliable. If they fail to forward a legal notice to you, you could face default judgments or legal penalties.
