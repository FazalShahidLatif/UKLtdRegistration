# Essential SIC Codes for Food & Beverage Exporters: Ensuring Compliance and Banking Success

In the world of international food trade, your **SIC Code** (Standard Industrial Classification) is more than just a number—it’s your business's "identity card" at **Companies House** and for every **high street bank** in the UK.

For exporters of **coffee**, **meat**, and **seafood**, choosing the right code is the first step toward a successful global launch.

## Top SIC Codes for the Food Industry

-   **46370**: Wholesale of **coffee**, tea, cocoa and spices.
-   **46320**: Wholesale of **meat** and meat products.
-   **46380**: Wholesale of other food, including fish (**seafood**), crustaceans and molluscs.
-   **46310**: Wholesale of fruit and vegetables.
-   **46390**: Non-specialised wholesale of food, beverages and tobacco.

## Why the Right SIC Code Matters for Banking
When you apply to **open a uk business bank account**, the bank's compliance software automatically checks your SIC code. If you are exporting **meat** but select a "General Trade" code, your application may be delayed or rejected for lack of specificity. A precise code proves you understand your market and its regulations.
