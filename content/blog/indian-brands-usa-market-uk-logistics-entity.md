# Scaling Your Indian Brand in the USA: Why UK-based Logistics and Entities Outperform Direct Exporting

The **USA market** is the ultimate goal for many Indian manufacturers of **leather wear**, **textiles**, and **fashion wear**. However, many Indian brands struggle to scale beyond small, infrequent orders due to the perceived risk of "long-distance" shipping and the lack of a local corporate presence.

Strategically, using a **UK based** limited company as your global headquarters is often more effective than exporting directly from India to the USA.

## The "London-to-New York" Corridor
The trade relationship between the UK and the USA is one of the strongest in the world. By positioning your Indian brand as a UK entity, you benefit from:
-   **Lower Barriers to Entry**: US customers and platforms like Amazon US are more familiar with UK corporate structures than Indian ones.
-   **Simplified Banking**: It is significantly easier to open a US-based merchant account (like Stripe US) using a UK company than an Indian one.
-   **Trust & Reliability**: A London **registered office address** gives your brand a western "home base" that reassures US retailers and consumers.

## SIC Codes for the US Market
When setting up your UK hub for US exports, ensure you use the correct industrial classifications:
-   **46410**: Wholesale of textiles.
-   **46420**: Wholesale of clothing and footwear.
-   **46370**: Wholesale of coffee, tea, cocoa and spices.

These codes are recognized globally and simplify the process of setting up international supply chain contracts.

### Logistics Hub: The UK Advantage
Many Indian brands use the UK as a distribution hub. You can ship bulk quantities of **leather wear** or **textiles** to the UK, and then ship smaller, faster orders to both the USA and Europe. This reduces shipping times and improves customer satisfaction in the highly competitive US market.
