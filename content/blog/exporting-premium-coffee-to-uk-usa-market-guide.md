# From Farm to Cup: Scaling Your Coffee Brand in the UK and USA via a London Entity

For coffee producers and exporters, the **USA and UK markets** are the gold standard. Whether you are exporting Arabica from Ethiopia, Robusta from Vietnam, or specialty blends from Brazil, the goal is to get closer to the final consumer.

By forming a **UK based** limited company, you can bridge the gap between the coffee farm and the metropolitan coffee shop.

## The "London Coffee" Strategy
London is one of the world's premier coffee hubs. Using a **registered office address** in London allows your coffee brand to:
-   **Partner with Boutique Roasters**: Local roasters prefer buying from UK entities to simplify their own accounting and VAT reclamation.
-   **Sell Direct-to-Consumer (D2C)**: Launch a UK-based e-commerce store and ship directly to coffee lovers across Europe and the USA.
-   **Manage Brand Identity**: A UK company allows you to trademark your brand in the West, protecting your intellectual property as you scale.

## Banking for Coffee Exporters
One of the primary benefits of a **UK business bank account** is multi-currency management. Coffee is traded in USD, but your costs might be in local currency and your UK sales in GBP. Modern platforms like **Wise Business** or **Revolut** allow you to hold and convert these currencies at the lowest possible cost, maximizing your profit per bag.

### SIC Code 46370: The Coffee Exporter's Standard
When you **form a uk company**, you must use SIC code **46370** (Wholesale of coffee, tea, cocoa and spices). This code is highly recognized by banks and ensures your **company registration** is perfectly aligned with your business activities.

## Earning Foreign Exchange
By capturing the retail value of your coffee in the UK and USA, you earn significantly more foreign exchange than by selling bulk green beans to international traders. Repatriating these earnings to your home country supports local farming communities and sustainable development.
