# Choosing the Perfect SIC Code for Agricultural and Food Exports to the EU

For a South Asian exporter specialized in **spices**, **textiles**, or **agricultural products**, the technical side of **uk company registration** is as important as the trade itself. A critical part of this is the **SIC Code** (Standard Industrial Classification), which defines your business activity to **Companies House** and the **HMRC**.

Selecting the right code is essential for VAT registration and for your ability to **open a uk business bank account**.

## Strategic SIC Codes for Agri & Food Exporters
If you are exporting traditional flavors and products from Bangladesh or India to the **European markets**, consider these codes:

-   **46370**: Wholesale of coffee, tea, cocoa and **spices**.
-   **46310**: Wholesale of fruit and vegetables.
-   **46390**: Non-specialised wholesale of food, beverages and tobacco.
-   **10840**: Manufacture of condiments and seasonings.

## Why the Right Code Matters for EU Trade
When you operate as a UK entity, you are often required to register for VAT to sell to EU businesses. Your SIC code helps the tax authorities understand the nature of your imports. For example, some agricultural products may have different VAT treatments. Having an accurate **SIC code** on your **company details** ensures you are taxed correctly from day one.

### Banking Compliance for South Asian Directors
**High street banks** and digital banking platforms use SIC codes to assess the "risk profile" of a new business. By selecting a standard wholesale code for **spices** or **textiles**, you prove that you are a legitimate trader, which significantly speeds up the process to **open a uk business bank account**.
