# Navigating SIC Codes for Pakistani Textile Manufacturers: Choosing the Right Category for Global Trade

For a Pakistani textile manufacturer in Faisalabad or Karachi, the decision to **form a uk company** is a strategic move to access **USA and European markets**. However, a common technical hurdle during the registration process is selecting the correct **SIC Code** (Standard Industrial Classification).

Your SIC code doesn't just tell Companies House what you do; it tells **high street banks** and international payment gateways like Stripe whether your business is high-risk or standard.

## Essential SIC Codes for the Textile Industry

If you are exporting **textiles**, **apparel**, or **fashion wear**, you should consider the following codes:

- **13200**: Weaving of textiles (Ideal for fabric manufacturers).
- **14131**: Manufacture of other men's outerwear (Perfect for fashion wear brands).
- **14132**: Manufacture of other women's outerwear.
- **46410**: Wholesale of textiles (The most common choice for exporters who don't manufacture *inside* the UK).

## Why Accuracy Matters for Pakistani Exporters
When you apply to **open a uk business bank account**, the bank will cross-reference your SIC code with your website and business plan. If you manufacture **leather wear** but select a "Consultancy" code, your account application could be rejected for inconsistency.

### Strategic Alignment with Global Standards
Using international SIC standards through a UK Ltd allows your Pakistani business to align with Western procurement systems. Large retailers in the EU often require their vendors to have clear industrial classifications that match their own supply chain requirements.
