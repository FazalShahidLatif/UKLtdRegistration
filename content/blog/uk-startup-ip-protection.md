# Intellectual Property: Protecting Your Brand Alongside Formation

## The Common Myth: "My Company Name is my Trademark"
Many founders believe that registering a name at Companies House provides legal protection for their brand. **This is a dangerous misconception**. Companies House merely prevents another *company* from using the exact same name; it does not prevent others from using the name in trade or registering it as a trademark.

## 1. What is a Trademark?
A trademark is a legal protection for your brand's unique identifiers:
- **Brand Names**
- **Logos & Symbols**
- **Slogans**
- **Product Shapes**

## 2. The Power of UK IPO Registration
To truly own your brand, you must register it with the **UK Intellectual Property Office (UK IPO)**. 
- **Effect**: Grants you the exclusive right to use the mark in your category (class) across the UK.
- **Asset Value**: A registered trademark is a tradable asset that increases the valuation of your company.

## 3. Synergy with Company Formation
We recommend a "Simultaneous Strike" approach:
1. **Search**: Check the Companies House register *and* the UK IPO trademark database.
2. **Form**: Register your Limited company.
3. **Protect**: Immediately apply for your primary trademarks to ensure no one else can exploit your goodwill.
